<p><p>A Real Time Clock (RTC) is a battery-powered device that keeps track of the current time and date, even when the main system is powered off. The RTC market features various types, primarily I2C and SPI RTCs. I2C RTCs utilize the Inter-Integrated Circuit protocol for efficient communication over two wires, while SPI RTCs leverage the Serial Peripheral Interface for faster data transfer through a four-wire connection. Other types may include UART and parallel RTCs, catering to diverse application requirements across electronics.</p></p>
