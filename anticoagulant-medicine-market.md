<p><p>The anticoagulant medicine market is divided into several key categories. NOACs (Novel Oral Anticoagulants) are newer medications that offer advantages such as fewer dietary restrictions and no need for regular monitoring. Heparin, an older antithrombotic, is often used in hospitals for rapid anticoagulation. Warfarin, a long-established oral anticoagulant, requires careful monitoring due to dietary interactions and variability in response. The “Others” category includes various less common anticoagulants and specialty products, catering to specific patient needs and clinical scenarios.</p></p>
