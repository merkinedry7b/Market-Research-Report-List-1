<p><p>The ophthalmic medical devices market is categorized into three classes based on risk and regulatory requirements. Class I devices, such as eye patches and glasses, are considered low risk and have minimal regulatory control. Class II devices, including contact lenses and intraocular lenses, pose moderate risk and require more extensive regulatory oversight, often involving performance standards. Class III devices, like ocular implants and surgical instruments, present high risk and necessitate rigorous testing and pre-market approval to ensure safety and efficacy.</p></p>
