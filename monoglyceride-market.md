<p><ul><li>General Monoglyceride</li><li>Distilled Monoglyceride</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The monoglyceride market comprises two primary types: general monoglycerides and distilled monoglycerides. General monoglycerides are produced through partial glycerolysis of triglycerides and are commonly used as emulsifiers in food products. Distilled monoglycerides, on the other hand, undergo additional purification processes, enhancing their quality and stability, making them suitable for more specific applications in both food and industrial sectors. The distinction lies in the degree of refinement, which affects their functionality and market applications.</p></p>
